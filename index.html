Hello! I'm your first clue!
I'm bright round and blue,
And bigger than you!

A little door
Under the floor!
I watch wheels role,
And passerby strole.

I'm loud, when I'm on!
The morning wakes me.
Adults drink the drink I make,
From all the beans that shake me.

I stand tall in my corner,
Now rounder and older,
Washed up on a shore
Many years before.

I live in a busy room,
But I like to keep things clean.
Can you find me?
I'm green.

I like all the attention,
So I sit in the middle.
I'm used for a lot:
Foot rest, drink holder, flower pot.

Red light, green light,
Clue light, you light!
Game winner gets a clue!

Now take your age and times it by 10,
Jump that many jacks, for a clue again!

You gather around me,
Tell spooky ghost stories.
I keep your hearts warm,
As you revel in glories.

A bone sits on top,
And a blue fish.
When you open me,
you'll get your wish!

I quake and I shake,
Rumble and tumble.
I make you look fresh
Even after big fumbles.

I'm round and pipey,
Solid, no stripey.
Wood chips all around,
I stick out of the ground.

I like to sneak,
To find me, you may need to peak.
I have only one name,
But play many games.

Be careful with me!
I'm fragile as can be,
But I do love to sing
When you draw on my strings.

Laying near a window sill,
I do like to bite,
But I sit quite still.

I don't drink water,
Or sit in a pot.
I look like a flower,
but I am not.
